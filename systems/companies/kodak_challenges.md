# Kodak Audit: Challenge Cases

## What would actually disprove the margin-collapse signal?

### Challenge 1: Deliberate Portfolio Transition
**Claim:** Margin squeeze from 2005-2010 was intentional.
- Kodak deliberately exited high-margin film to fund low-margin digital scale.
- This looks like False Acceleration (margin compression) but is actually strategic.
- **Test:** Did Kodak leadership explicitly communicate this trade-off to investors? Were margins expected to recover post-transition?
- **Evidence needed:** Earnings call transcripts, strategy documents.

### Challenge 2: Timing Misdiagnosis
**Claim:** Margins stayed healthy longer than the audit claims.
- If operating margins were still 12%+ through 2009, the signal fires too early.
- False alarm on "imminent bankruptcy" signals.
- **Test:** Get actual quarterly margin data 2005-2012.
- **Evidence needed:** SEC filings, 10-K reports.

### Challenge 3: The Real Problem Was Market Timing, Not Internal Decay
**Claim:** Kodak had real digital capabilities but exited before monetizing.
- Inkjet printing business actually had potential (HP eventually dominated this).
- Digital imaging sensors were viable (Sony commercialized successfully).
- Kodak failed due to timing/execution, not signal decay.
- **Test:** Could these businesses have succeeded with different capital allocation or market timing?
- **Evidence needed:** Analyst reports, patent filings, product roadmap docs.

### Challenge 4: Revenue Stability Masks True Demand Destruction
**Claim:** $10B revenue was held up by government contracts, not film demand.
- If core film revenue dropped 50% but government contracts up 40%, margins still show decay.
- But the "growth_source" attribution is wrong—this is not False Acceleration, it's business model shift.
- **Test:** Break down revenue by segment (film, digital, government, services).
- **Evidence needed:** Segment revenue from 10-K filings.

### Challenge 5: The Margin Story is Accounting, Not Economics
**Claim:** Reported margins don't reflect economic reality.
- Goodwill write-downs, restructuring charges, pension obligations masked real operating performance.
- True operating margin (ex-charges) might have been healthier than reported.
- **Test:** Calculate adjusted EBITDA separately from reported operating income.
- **Evidence needed:** Non-GAAP reconciliations in earnings reports.
