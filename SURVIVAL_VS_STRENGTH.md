# Survival vs. Strength — Critical Distinction

**Date:** 2026-06-23

**Core insight:** An object can be strong (well-supported) and fragile (untested), or weak (little evidence) and durable (survived attack).

---

## Two Orthogonal Dimensions

### Strength

**Question:** How much evidence supports this object?

**Scale:**
- High: Multiple independent confirmations across cases
- Medium: Some evidence; competing with other explanations
- Low: Sparse evidence; heavily dependent on single case

**What it measures:** Evidentiary weight

**Example:** Evidence Class Taxonomy has HIGH strength because Class C scarcity is confirmed across 5 independent cases.

---

### Survival

**Question:** Has this object avoided being killed?

**Scale:**
- High: Has faced direct attack; falsification conditions approached; still standing
- Medium: Compression-tested; not yet directly attacked
- Low: Has been wounded or refuted

**What it measures:** Vulnerability to falsification

**Example:** PP#002 has HIGH survival because it's been compression-tested (found in Kodak) without being wounded by direct falsification.

## Why Survival Through Attack Matters

A high-strength object is only informative if it has survived genuine attempts to kill it.

Without those attempts, strength is indistinguishable from confirmation bias.

With those attempts, strength becomes trustworthy.

**Example:**

| Scenario | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| Taxonomy: 5-case support, never attacked | Fragile. Probably inflated. |
| Taxonomy: 5-case support, attacked in 10 cases, still standing in 8 | Robust. Earned its confidence. |

The second Taxonomy is objectively weaker (8/10 vs. 5/5) but epistemically stronger. It's survived because it resisted falsi­fication, not because it was never tested.

## The Most Important Calibration

> The repository is collecting opportunities for interpretations to fail.

Not: "Can we prove this right?"

But: "Can we find where this breaks?"

That shift in posture changes what counts as success.

**Success is not:** Finding more evidence for the object

**Success is:** Finding the wound and documenting that it didn't land

A surviving object becomes informative *because it had the chance to die and didn't.*
