## Why This Matters

Research program makes claims → Claims examined immediately → Gaps identified → Claims demoted → Only observation recorded

## Distinguishing Two Kinds of Research Programs

### Type A: Good At Generating Ideas

- Creates frameworks ✓
- Creates hypotheses ✓
- Creates pressure points ✓
- Creates meta-hypotheses ✓

**But:** Does not reliably identify when ideas exceed evidence

**Risk:** Accumulates over-claims disguised as findings

### Type B: Good At Demoting Ideas

- Creates frameworks ✓
- Tests frameworks ✓
- Identifies gaps between evidence and claim ✓
- Demotes claims when gaps are large ✓
- Records demotion explicitly ✓

**Risk:** Lower (built-in correction mechanism)

**Value:** Higher (claims that survive demotion are more trustworthy)

**Current repository:** Increasingly showing Type B behavior

---

## What Makes Demotion Harder Than Promotion

**Promoting an idea:** 
- Find supporting evidence
- Articulate clearly
- Publish/record
- Done

**Demoting an idea:**
- Identify that claim exceeds evidence
- Override the appeal of the claim
- Admit over-reaching
- Record demotion explicitly
- Maintain humility about what is actually known

**Demotion requires:**
- Resistance to narrative momentum
- Comfort with incompleteness
- Willingness to say "unknown"
- Disciplined gap-checking

**This is rarer.**

## Why Behaviors Trump Objects

**Frameworks can be wrong.**
- Objects fail
- Assumptions change
- Evidence contradicts

**Behaviors can persist.**
- Demotion discipline works regardless of which claims are true
- Self-correction improves outcomes even if individual objects fail
- Culture of gap-checking survives framework revision

**Long-term repository health depends more on:**
- Does it reliably demote over-claims?

**Than on:**
- Do individual frameworks survive?

---

## The Question That Matters

Not: "Which objects will survive Phase 3?"

But: "Will the repository continue to demote itself when appropriate?"

**Because:**
- Objects can be wrong; behaviors prevent wrongness from metastasizing
- Frameworks can be overfitted; behaviors catch overfitting
- Claims can exceed evidence; behaviors measure the gap
- Traditions can corrupt; behaviors resist corruption

## What To Watch Going Forward

**Early warning sign of drift:**
- Frameworks are promoted
- Over-claims are defended
- Demotion process slows
- Gaps between evidence and claim increase

**Sign of health:**
- Frameworks are tested
- Over-claims are identified quickly
- Demotion is explicit
- Repository remains uncomfortable with what is claimed
