# Hypothesis H2: Execution Gap

## Candidate Hypothesis

Systems may fail despite visible signals when execution capacity cannot adapt to reality at the necessary pace.

## Distinction from Ω

Ω detects: Signal decay (margins, demand, market share)

H2 proposes: A failure mode where signal IS visible but organizational response lags or fails

## Evidence from Kodak

- Signal visible: Film demand declining, digital threat recognized by CEO (1993+)
- Action capacity: Limited by:
  - Middle management film-centric routines (resisted pivot)
  - Half-hearted execution (digital efforts "poorly executed")
  - Timing lag (investments made but too late to scale)
- Manifestation: Market share collapse (27% → 7%), accelerating losses

Candidate only. Requires pattern confirmation from additional cases.
