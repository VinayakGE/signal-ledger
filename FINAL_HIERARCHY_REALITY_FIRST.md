# Final Hierarchy: Reality First

**Date:** 2026-06-23

**Compression:** Hierarchy clarified to prevent next-order error

---

## The Hierarchy

```
Reality
  ↓
Investigation
  ↓
Objects
  ↓
Methodology
```

**Principle:** When something higher conflicts with something lower, the lower thing loses.

---

## What This Means

### Reality Wounds Object
Object loses. Always.

Example:
- Object predicts: "Class C evidence will be scarce"
- Reality shows: "Class C evidence is abundant"
- Consequence: Object is wounded
- Action: Record the wound; learn why

### Investigation Contradicts Methodology
Methodology loses. Always.

Example:
- Methodology says: "Pre-register falsification conditions"
- Investigation reveals: "The most important finding is something not predicted by any condition"
- Consequence: Methodology was incomplete
- Action: Revise methodology to capture unexpected discoveries

### Evidence Contradicts Explanation
Explanation loses. Always.

Example:
- Explanation says: "Neumann believed the model would scale to profitability"
- Evidence shows: "Contemporaneous documents reveal Neumann expected loss-leading indefinitely"
- Consequence: Explanation is wrong
- Action: Update understanding; don't defend explanation

### Objects Serve Investigation, Not Vice Versa
Example (Wrong): "WeWork investigation tests whether PP#002 survives"
Example (Right): "WeWork investigation explores organizational failure. PP#002 helps organize observations."

---

## Three Questions for WeWork (In Priority Order)

### Priority 1: What Is True?

**First question, before anything else:**

What is actually the case about WeWork's failure and Neumann's decision-making?

**How to answer:**
- Observe without object lens
- Classify evidence by type and date
- Document what is demonstrably true
- Mark what remains unknowable

**Success criterion:** Can the investigation stand independent of Phase 2 objects? Yes.

**Guard rail:** Don't answer this through the lens of what would be surprising or would validate objects. Answer it through the lens of reality.

### Priority 2: What Is Surprising?

**Second question, after truth is established:**

Given what is true about WeWork, what contradicts expectations?

**How to answer:**
- Compare findings to Phase 1 hypotheses
- Compare findings to external theory
- Identify patterns that weren't predicted
- Highlight genuine discoveries

**Success criterion:** Can you identify something WeWork reveals that the repository didn't already know? Yes.

**Guard rail:** Don't mistake "surprising to us because we didn't think about it" for "true." Reality determines truth; surprise is secondary.

### Priority 3: What Does This Mean For Objects?

**Third question, after truth and surprise are established:**

Given what is true and surprising about WeWork, how do Phase 2 objects perform?

**How to answer:**
- Apply pre-registered falsification conditions
- Score objects on Survival/Strength/Generativity
- Connect findings to M1 and meta-hypothesis
- Determine next direction

**Success criterion:** Objects are scored honestly based on what investigation revealed. Yes.

**Guard rail:** Don't let object implications change assessment of what is true or what is surprising.

## The Hidden Symmetry (The Next Danger)

### Earlier Danger: Protecting Methodology
**Error:** Methodology becomes sacred; evidence gets rationalized
**Symptom:** "Our methodology is sound; results must show it works"
**Result:** Repository stops learning; starts defending

### Next Danger: Protecting Surprise
**Error:** Surprise becomes sacred; truth gets rationalized
**Symptom:** "Interesting findings should win; less interesting truths should lose"
**Result:** Repository becomes addicted to being surprised; distorts reality

### How This Happens
"Interesting" starts winning over "true"
- Repository values discovery
- Discovery requires surprise
- Surprise becomes the goal
- Truth becomes secondary to interestingness
- Evidence gets shaped to maximize surprise

### The Guard Rail
**Question 1 always gets answered first, purely, rigorously:** What is true?

Only after: What is surprising?

If truth is boring, that's still truth.

## The Highest-Information Outcome

### Current Repository Expectation

- **Explanations:** Fragile (die under pressure)
- **Boundaries:** Durable (survive pressure)
- **Instruments:** Durable (survive pressure)

### What Would Be Highest-Information

A genuinely durable explanatory object appearing in WeWork.

Why?

**Because it would:**
1. Wound M1 (prediction: explanations die faster; here's one that doesn't)
2. Be surprising (contradicts expectation; portfolio-changing)
3. Be attackable (could itself be wounded; testable)
4. Be valuable (teaches WHEN explanations survive, not whether they do)

### Why This Is Better Than Another Surviving Boundary

| Outcome | Information Value |
|---------|------------------|
| Another boundary survives | Low (predictable, confirms pattern) |
| Boundary is wounded | Medium (teaches domain-specificity) |
| Durable explanation appears | High (teaches when explanations survive; wounds M1) |

**The highest-information result is the one that surprises the repository most while being true.**

Not the one that validates predictions.

---

## What This Means for Investigation Discipline

### Wrong Posture
1. "What would make our objects look best?"
2. "What surprises would validate our framework?"
3. "How can we interpret findings to support predictions?"

### Right Posture
1. "What is actually true about WeWork?"
2. "What does reality reveal that we didn't expect?"
3. "How do findings affect our understanding?"

The second posture serves reality.

The first serves objects.

---

## The Question Remains Open

> Which kinds of knowledge about organizational failure survive the adversarial pressure of investigation?

**This is genuinely open.**

If the answer was already known, there would be no reason to run WeWork.

Possible answers:
- Primarily boundaries (limits on what can be known)
- Primarily instruments (how to investigate systematically)
- Primarily explanations (why things happened)
- Some mix depending on context
- Something the repository hasn't discovered yet

**Any of these answers is valuable.**

The goal is to discover which is true.

Not to discover which is interesting.

## Implementation: Hold Three Questions Simultaneously

**During WeWork investigation:**

| Priority | Question | Answered By | Guard Rail |
|----------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| 1 | What is true? | Evidence + Classification | Truth before surprise |
| 2 | What is surprising? | Comparison to expectations | Surprise after truth |
| 3 | What does this mean for objects? | Applying frameworks | Objects after findings |

**The temptation will be to invert them.**

Watch for:
- Prioritizing "This would be surprising" over "Is this true?"
- Dismissing true findings because they're not interesting
- Defending surprise when evidence contradicts it

---

## The Sentence That Captures It

> The investigation serves reality. The objects serve the investigation.

**This means:**
- Reality comes first (is this actually true?)
- Investigation serves reality (let's understand what's true)
- Objects serve investigation (these tools help us understand)
- Methodology serves objects (these methods help us use the tools)

**When anything conflicts:**
- Methodology can lose to objects
- Objects can lose to investigation
- Investigation can lose to reality

**Reality never loses.**

---

## Why This Matters for Phase 2

Phase 1 risked defending explanations ("Kodak failed because...")

Phase 2 risks defending boundaries ("Motivation remains opaque because...")

The next risk is defending surprise ("This finding is interesting because...")

Each time, the danger is the same: prioritizing something downstream over reality.

The discipline is the same: Let reality win. Always.

---

## What Success Looks Like for WeWork

**Not:** "Our objects survived; our methodology was right"

**But:** "WeWork revealed what is true. Some of that was surprising. Some of our objects performed well; some were wounded. The repository learned something about which kinds of knowledge survive investigation."

**Success = Learning what is true, even if it's not what we predicted.**

Reality first.
