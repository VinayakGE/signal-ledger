## Methodological Advancement

### Evidence Class Taxonomy

Three classes established to classify what evidence is available about organizational decisions:

**Class A — Structural Evidence**

*What happened?*

- Proposal exists
- Proposal reviewed
- Proposal rejected

Characteristics:
- High confidence
- Recoverable from contemporaneous reporting
- Does not bridge motivation gap

Example: BBM cross-platform proposal formally developed, pitched to carriers, killed by Heins + Lazaridis

---

**Class B — Retrospective Explanation**

*Why participants later say it happened*

- Interviews years after event
- Memoirs
- Post-hoc recollections

Characteristics:
- Useful for hypothesis generation
- Outcome-contaminated (decision-maker knows outcome when explaining)
- Outcome knowledge may have restructured memory of motivation
- Cannot be checked against conditions at time T

Example: Balsillie's 2013 statement on why he left the board

---

**Class C — Contemporaneous Motivation**

*Why decision-makers believed it at time T*

- Board memos
- Internal emails
- Strategy notes
- Decision rationale written before outcome known

Characteristics:
- Only evidence class that genuinely bridges Zone A
- Rare
- Can be checked against contemporaneous conditions
- Produced before outcome knowledge contaminated memory

Example: A January 2012 board memo stating reasoning for rejecting BBM (does not currently exist in available sources)
