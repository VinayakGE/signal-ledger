## Object #2: PP#002 — Observable Selection, Opaque Motivation

**Definition:** An organizational selection (choice between alternatives) may be structurally observable while the motivation for that selection remains unrecoverable from available public evidence.

### Kodak #001

**Does it appear?** Possibly (requires reclassification)

**Potential evidence:**
- Observable: Kodak invested $3B+ in digital R&D 1993-2005; film revenue declined; R&D did not scale to market share
- Selection visible: Multiple strategic choices — Film-first protection vs. digital pivot; traditional business model vs. licensing model
- Opaque: Why leadership chose film protection despite R&D investment. Why the disconnect between investment and conviction?

**Current classification in Kodak:** Not explicitly tested for motivation opacity. Audit classifies as "Response Lag," not "Observable/Opaque."

**Could test without new investigation:** Did Kodak's leadership believe in the digital pivot (Class C evidence from board memos 1995-2000) or were they protecting film business against market pressure? This would reveal whether Kodak shows Zone A opacity like BlackBerry and GE.

**Latent strength:** High. Kodak's margin signal (20% → 5%) without proportional revenue loss suggests structural divergence — investment conviction vs. operational priority. This is precisely PP#002 structure.

---

### Nokia #002

**Does it appear?** Latent

**Potential evidence:**
- Observable: Samsung adopted Android early; Nokia delayed; both companies saw OHA formation
- Selection visible: Different strategic choices from same information environment
- Opaque: Why Nokia's leadership chose Symbian-first despite Samsung's faster Android response. Why co-CEO governance did not produce faster pivot (parallel to BlackBerry co-CEO divergence).

**Current classification:** Not explicitly mapped for Zone A/B boundary.

**Could test without new investigation:** Nokia board materials, Kallasvuo vs. Ollila decision records (if accessible) would reveal whether leadership understood the threat and chose differently, or whether threat understanding diverged. This is the same Zone A question as BlackBerry.

**Latent strength:** High. Nokia/Samsung comparison is structurally perfect for PP#002 testing — same market, different selections, different outcomes.

---

### WeWork #003

**Does it appear?** Latent (highest probability among uninvestigated cases)

**Potential evidence:**
- Observable: Neumann's public narrative (scalable real-estate model, profitable growth path) vs. private investor communications (burn rate, unit economics)
- Selection visible: Series E / Series F round decisions; IPO timing decision; governance structure decisions
- Opaque: Whether Neumann genuinely believed narrative or was sustaining capital flows; whether investors selected Neumann despite doubts or were themselves deceived

**Audit classification:** "Not yet investigated." But WeWork's founder-narrative structure makes this case **exceptionally likely** to produce independent PP#002 without deliberate hunting.

**Latent strength:** Highest among remaining cases. Zone A opacity in founder-led companies may work differently than in public institutions (GE, BlackBerry). Neumann's private journals, if they exist, would be Class C gold.

---

### GE #004

**Does it appear?** Yes

**Evidence:**
- Observable: Immelt's power-segment assumptions vs. Flannery's assumption revisions; Alstom integration facts available
- Selection visible: Immelt's capital allocation decisions 2010-2016; Flannery's rapid business-unit separation 2017
- Opaque: Whether Immelt understood the power-market structural shift and chose to continue investing, or whether the shift was not yet visible to him. This is the core H2-C question.

**Current classification:** Under attack (H2-C). Zone A/B boundary not yet explicit, but H2-C-E02 suggests information integration failure, which is zone A opacity at the model level.

**Strength:** Moderate (blocked by network policy on Class C primary sources).

---

### BlackBerry #005

**Does it appear?** Yes (primary case)

**Evidence:**
- Observable: BBM cross-platform alternative proposed, developed, pitched, killed; decision mechanism documented; Balsillie's departure direct consequence
- Selection visible: Clear institutional coalition (Heins + Lazaridis) against alternative
- Opaque: Why Lazaridis, who privately recognized "competing with a Mac" in 2007, supported killing the platform alternative in 2012. Lazaridis explicitly "declined to speak about board matters."

**Strength:** Strong. Zone A/B boundary explicitly mapped. Falsification condition defined.

## Object #4: Zone A/B Boundary

**Definition:** Systematic distinction between observable organizational structure and opaque motivation.

### Kodak #001

**Does it appear?** Latent

**Potential evidence:**
- Zone B (Observable): R&D spending decisions, business-unit allocation, margin signals over time
- Zone A (Opaque): Why leadership chose film protection despite mounting evidence; why R&D investment did not translate to strategic priority shift

**Current classification:** Not explicitly mapped. Audit describes as "Response Lag" (observable) without distinguishing what remains opaque about the response choice.

**Could test without new investigation:** Kodak's divergence between investment (signals R&D priority) and execution (film-first revenue protection) suggests Zone A opacity. Explicit mapping would clarify whether this is the same structure as BlackBerry/GE.

**Latent strength:** High. Margin collapse (observable) occurs while strategic direction remains unclear (opaque). This is precisely Zone A/B structure.

---

### Nokia #002

**Does it appear?** Latent

**Potential evidence:**
- Zone B (Observable): Samsung's OHA choice vs. Nokia's Symbian choice; both companies observed same market signal; different strategic responses documented
- Zone A (Opaque): Why Nokia chose Symbian-first despite threat recognition; whether this reflects genuine conviction or structural constraint

**Current classification:** Not explicitly mapped. But U5/U8 investigations implicitly explore this boundary (Samsung's information vs. Nokia's decision-making structure).

**Could test without new investigation:** Nokia/Samsung comparison is structurally perfect for Zone A/B mapping. Public statements and strategic announcements document Zone B. Private motivations remain Zone A unless board materials become accessible.

**Latent strength:** High. Different companies, same market, different selections = perfect test case.

### WeWork #003

- Zone B (Observable): Neumann's public statements, investor presentations, IPO narrative
