# Phase 2 Operating Doctrine

## The Scarcity Shift

**Phase 1 scarcity:** Cases

**Phase 2 scarcity:** Compression

This single shift changes every optimization target.

---

## Default Question Shift

**Phase 1 default question:**
> What should I investigate next?

This naturally creates expansion.

**Phase 2 default question:**
> What is the highest-leverage surviving uncertainty?

This naturally creates compression.

---

## Current Highest-Leverage Surviving Uncertainty

**Question:** Did the enterprise-extension model undergo genuine reconsideration between PlayBook reception and January 2012?

**Why high leverage:**

| Object | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| H4 (Model Failure) | Direct — wound or strengthen |

One answer changes multiple objects simultaneously. That is leverage.

## Phase 2 Session Structure

### Session N: Focused Artifact Collection

**Scope:** BlackBerry model-reconsideration attack

**Stop condition:** 3-5 artifacts specifically addressing model reconsideration, then stop

**Do not:** Continue collecting indefinitely. Stop at defined condition.

**Expected outcomes:**
- Evidence of reconsideration → H4 wounded, H5 reshaped, PP#002 pressured
- Evidence of absence → H4 strengthened, map clarifies

Both are valuable. Either outcome completes the attack.

---

- How many active constraints exist?
- How many pressure points exist?
- Which objects have survived multiple cases?

|--------|--------------|--------|
| ? | ? | ? |

**Critical discipline:** Audit is inventory. Not synthesis. Not promotion. Not topology creation. Just: What exists? What survived? What is still provisional?

## Guardrails Against Phase 1 Drift

### Do Not

- Open Case #006 before audit completes
- Return to GE before understanding current inventory
- Create new methodology files (surface area is adequate)
- Invent topologies (wait until survival is clear)
- Perform synthesis during audit (audit = inventory only)
- Create new objects during audit (identification only)

### Do

- Complete defined BlackBerry attack, then stop
- Conduct inventory audit without synthesis
- Answer "What survived?" before asking "What should we do next?"
- Keep the default question: "Highest-leverage surviving uncertainty?"

---

## Why This Matters

In Phase 1, a sixth investigation may have been the highest-leverage next step.

In Phase 2, unresolved compression work is usually the scarcer asset.

A case added before compression work completes is expansion competing with compression.

Phase 2 optimizes for compression first, expansion second.

---

## The Canonical Phase 2 Principle

Do not ask what was investigated.

Ask what survived investigation.

Every decision in Phase 2 flows from that question.
