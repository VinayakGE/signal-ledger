## Phase 2: Instrumentation

**Begins:** Session of 2026-06-21

**Marker:** Evidence Class Taxonomy

The first object that is:
- Reusable across multiple cases
- Not specific to one investigation
- Applicable to future work without modification
- A measurement instrument, not a lesson

**Process:**
- Stress explanations (expose limitations)
- Discover boundaries (where knowing stops)
- Build measurement instruments (frameworks for evaluating future evidence)

**Output type:**
- Pressure points with falsification conditions (falsifiable, not just observable)
- Measurement instruments (evidence class taxonomy, similar frameworks)
- Constraints derived from boundary-finding
- Meta-level observations about organizational visibility

**Characteristic question:**
"What is the highest-leverage uncertainty still under pressure?"

---

## The Shift

**Phase 1 asks:** What do we observe?

**Phase 2 asks:** What can we trust about what we observe?

Phase 1 is about accumulating data.
Phase 2 is about validating methodology.

A young research program must do Phase 1 first. But once Phase 1 produces enough material, Phase 2 becomes more valuable than continuing Phase 1.

## Why This Sequence

**Finish BlackBerry first:**
- It's the live thread with highest remaining leverage
- Walking away now leaves the test half-finished
- PP#002 won't be properly validated until this test is complete

**Audit before expanding:**
- Most young research programs fail by adding cases without consolidating learning
- 5 cases + 2 pressure points + 1 measurement instrument is enough material for genuine audit
- An audit will reveal whether the next case should be GE, Case #006, or something else entirely

**Decide from consolidated position:**
- After audit, the leverage point becomes clear
- Prevents premature case multiplication
- Allows strategic thinking instead of reactive momentum-following

## What Phase 2 Means

The repository is no longer primarily in learning mode.

It is now in validation mode.

This is healthier for a program that has been running for several months. At some point, adding new data becomes less valuable than understanding what the existing data means.

Phase 2 is that transition.

**The repository has spent months learning from companies. It may be time to spend a session learning from itself.**
