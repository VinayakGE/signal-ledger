### Option 3: Repository Audit (Highest Long-Term Value)

**Advantage:** Consolidates learning before expansion. Prevents premature case multiplication.

Stop investigating. Map what has survived pressure:

- Which observations recur across cases?
- Which died (contradicted by evidence)?
- Which narrowed (refined to smaller claims)?
- Which remain genuinely open?

**Why valuable:**
Most young research programs fail by adding cases instead of consolidating learning. Five cases is enough to identify early patterns. A sixth case without consolidation may increase activity without increasing learning.

**Process:**
- Audit Kodak: what did it establish? What is still contingent?
- Audit Nokia: which hypotheses collapsed? Which remain viable?
- Audit WeWork: state-space was refined; how did that happen? What would break it?
- Audit GE: what is the actual load-bearing question? What would resolve it?
- Audit BlackBerry: does PP#002 survive scrutiny? What would wound it?

**Expected outcome:**
Precise map of what the repository has actually learned vs. what it assumes.
