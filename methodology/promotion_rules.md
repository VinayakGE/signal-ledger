# Promotion Rules

## Purpose

Signal Ledger separates:

- Observations
- Hypotheses
- Topologies

Promotion requires evidence.

Interesting ideas do not earn promotion.

---

## Levels

### Level 0 — Observation

A recorded fact.

Examples:

- Market share declined.
- Operating margins compressed.
- Customer churn increased.

Observations make no causal claim.

---

### Level 1 — Hypothesis

A proposed explanation.

Examples:

- Signal visible, response lagged.
- Incentives corrupted feedback.
- Organizational paralysis emerged.

Hypotheses are candidates.

They are not findings.

---

### Level 2 — Provisional Topology

Requirements:

- Minimum 3 independent investigations.
- Similar causal structure observed.
- No major contradictory case.

Status: Provisional.

Additional investigations may still overturn it.

---

### Level 3 — Established Topology

Requirements:

- Minimum 10 independent investigations.
- Multiple domains represented.
- Repeated predictive usefulness.
- Survives contradictory testing.

Status: Established.

Still subject to revision.

---

## Demotion

Any topology may be demoted if:

- Contradictory evidence emerges.
- New investigations fail replication.
- Better explanations appear.

Promotion is not permanent.

---

## Burden of Proof

The burden of proof increases at each level.

| Level | Burden |
|-------|--------|
| Observation | Minimal |
| Hypothesis | Moderate |
| Provisional Topology | High |
| Established Topology | Very High |

---

## Candidate Topologies

Candidate topologies may exist in `hypotheses/`.

They remain candidates until promotion criteria are satisfied.

Examples:

- Signal Visible, Response Lagged
- Incentive Corruption
- Organizational Paralysis
- External Discontinuity
- Industry Blindness

These names do not imply validation.

They only identify recurring possibilities worth testing.

---

## Core Principle

Rows earn features.

Investigations earn hypotheses.

Hypotheses earn topologies.

Nothing earns promotion through intuition alone.
