# Audit Protocol

## Purpose

Signal Ledger exists to investigate whether increasing fragility could have been detected before outcomes became obvious.

The goal is not to explain history.

The goal is to determine whether observable signals existed before the outcome.

---

## Investigation Workflow

Every investigation follows the same sequence:

1. Define the question.
2. Freeze competing hypotheses.
3. Gather evidence.
4. Gather contradictory evidence.
5. Record unknowns.
6. Assign confidence.
7. Classify.

Conclusions are earned at the end of the process.

They are never assumed at the beginning.

---

## Required Sections

### Research Question

A specific, falsifiable question.

Example:

> "Could a disciplined observer have detected increasing fragility before the outcome became obvious?"

---

### Competing Hypotheses

At least two competing explanations.

Hypotheses must make different predictions.

No investigation may begin with a single favored explanation.

---

### Evidence For

Evidence supporting each hypothesis.

Evidence must be linked to observations.

Interpretations should be separated from facts.

---

### Evidence Against

Evidence contradicting each hypothesis.

Contradictory evidence is mandatory.

An investigation without contradictory evidence is incomplete.

---

### Unknowns

Open questions that materially affect confidence.

Unknowns are not weaknesses.

Unknowns are part of the result.

---

### Confidence

Low / Medium / High

Confidence reflects evidence quality, not confidence in the outcome.

---

## Valid Outcomes

The following are valid outcomes:

- Supports H1
- Supports H2
- Supports H3
- Mixed evidence
- Unresolved
- No finding

"No finding" is a successful investigation.

---

## Prohibited Behaviors

Do not:

- Start with a conclusion.
- Search only for supporting evidence.
- Promote hypotheses during investigation.
- Modify hypotheses after seeing evidence.
- Remove contradictory evidence.

---

## Guiding Principle

Observations first.

Explanations second.

Conclusions last.
