---

## Evidence Class Taxonomy (Reference)

### Class A — Structural Evidence

**Question answered:** What happened?

- Observable sequence: Proposal exists → Proposal reviewed → Proposal rejected
- High confidence
- Recoverable from contemporaneous reporting
- Does NOT bridge motivation gap

### Class B — Retrospective Explanation

**Question answered:** Why participants later say it happened

- Interviews years after event
- Memoirs, post-hoc recollections
- Outcome-contaminated (decision-maker knows outcome when explaining)
- Outcome knowledge may have restructured memory of motivation
- Cannot check against conditions at time T

### Class C — Contemporaneous Motivation

**Question answered:** Why decision-makers believed it at time T

- Board memos, internal emails, strategy notes
- Decision rationale written before outcome known
- Only evidence class that genuinely bridges Zone A
- Rare
- Can check against contemporaneous conditions

## Kodak #001 — Evidence Distribution

### Class A (Structural) — Strong

**Kodak's observable facts:**

| Event | Confidence | Source |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Invented digital camera 1975 | High | Historical record, press |
| $3B+ R&D investment 1993-2005 | High | SEC 10-K filings |
| Operating margin 20% (2000) → 5% (2008) | High | Annual reports |
| Revenue stable $10B (1990s-2010) | High | SEC filings |
| 2005 strategic announcement: "Digital > Traditional" | High | Press releases, investor calls |
| Digital revenue <10% of total 2005-2010 | High | Segment reporting |
| Bankruptcy 2012 | High | Public record |

**Kodak assessment at Class A:** ✓ Strong case. Structural facts are well-documented and recoverable.

**What Class A establishes:** Kodak recognized digital threat, invested heavily, announced pivot, failed to execute. The observable sequence is clear.

**What Class A does NOT establish:** Why investment didn't scale. Why announcement/execution diverged. Why film protection persisted despite margin signal.

---

### Class B (Retrospective) — Medium to Weak

**Kodak's available retrospective evidence:**

| Source | Availability | Status |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Post-bankruptcy executive interviews | Limited | Some executives available; outcome-contaminated |
| Kodak business histories (2013+) | Some | Secondary sources; relying on partial interviews |
| Memoirs from Kodak leaders | Sparse | Limited public memoirs; most executives did not write major retrospectives |

**Kodak assessment at Class B:** Medium availability; outcome contamination risk is high.

**Why contamination matters:** Executives explaining Kodak's failure post-bankruptcy will rationally reconstruct narratives to explain the outcome. They cannot report what they believed in 1998 without that knowledge being filtered through 2012-2020 hindsight.

**Example of contamination risk:**
- Retrospective (2015): "We should have seen that digital would dominate"
- Contemporaneous reality (1995): Digital margins were unclear; desktop photography was emerging but not yet dominant

**Kodak assessment:** Class B evidence available but weak for establishing contemporaneous beliefs.

---

### Class C (Contemporaneous Motivation) — Inaccessible

**Kodak's needed Class C evidence:**

| Document Type | Likely Existence | Accessibility |
|---|---|---|
| Board minutes 1993-2005 | Very likely | Probably not public |
| CEO strategy memos 1995-2010 | Very likely | Probably not public |
| Capital committee decisions on film/digital | Very likely | Probably not public |
| Internal strategy documents | Very likely | Probably not public |

**Kodak assessment at Class C:** Zero public accessibility.

**Why inaccessible:** Kodak is defunct (bankrupt 2012). Private company archives are not typically preserved as public record unless donated to institutions. Kodak's board materials would be sensitive (internal strategic disputes).

**What we would learn from Class C:** Whether 1998 board was debating film-protection actively (would show H4 conviction) or whether the decision was structural/unavoidable (would show H5 constraint).

**Current status:** Cannot establish this distinction without Class C evidence.

## Compression Work Complete: Three Critical Insights

### Insight 1: Portfolio Is Strong at Class A (Observable Structure)

All five cases have clear, well-documented observable structure. The "what happened" question can be reliably established across the portfolio.

### Insight 2: Portfolio Is Weak at Class B (Retrospective)

All five cases suffer from outcome contamination in retrospective evidence. Survivors explain decisions through lens of failure. Systematic bias prevents reliable establishment of contemporaneous beliefs.

### Insight 3: Portfolio Hits Epistemic Boundary at Class C (Contemporaneous Motivation)

Zone A (motivation, conviction, constraints) remains **systematically inaccessible** across the portfolio.

- Kodak: Bankrupt; archives not public
- Nokia: Public company, partial access; board-level decisions private
- WeWork: Private company; records may not exist or be accessible
- GE: Network policy blocks access
- BlackBerry: Primary decision-maker explicitly declined

**This is not a gap. This is a boundary.**

## Implication for Phase 2: Evidence-Class Discipline Required

Going forward, all investigations must:

1. **Classify evidence type before claiming resolution**
   - Class A claims: "What happened" — can be strong
   - Class B claims: "Why participants say it happened" — mark as outcome-contaminated
   - Class C claims: "Why it happened at time T" — rare; preserve as highest-value if found

2. **Acknowledge Zone A opacity explicitly**
   - If asking motivation questions → acknowledge Class C unavailability
   - If relying on retrospective → explicitly note outcome contamination
   - If Zone A opaque → say so; don't infer

3. **Validate measurement instruments at Class A level**
   - PP#001 (Backward Excavation) — observable at Class A across portfolio ✓
   - PP#002 (Observable Selection, Opaque Motivation) — observable at Class A across portfolio ✓
   - Zone A/B boundary — observable at Class A across portfolio ✓

4. **Expect Zone A opacity as normal**
   - Observable organizational structure ≠ interpretable organizational motivation
   - This is a feature of institutional complexity, not a research failure
   - PP#002 and Zone A/B are valid precisely because they acknowledge this boundary
