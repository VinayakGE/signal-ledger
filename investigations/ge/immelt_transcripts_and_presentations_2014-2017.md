# Immelt Earnings Call Transcripts & Investor Presentations (2014-2017)

## Research Objective
Locate and retrieve complete earnings call transcripts and investor presentations from 2014-2017, specifically targeting Q3 2015, Q4 2016, and Q2 2017, to extract evidence of Immelt's statements on:
1. Renewable energy growth/acceleration
2. Gas/coal demand forecasts
3. Power generation market structure
4. Overcapacity or market weakness

## Critical Evidence Summary

### Model Assumption: "Demand Will Track Global Economic Growth"

**Key Finding:** GE's central assumption underlying the Alstom acquisition was that demand for natural gas and coal power would continue to track global economic growth. This assumption contradicted publicly available renewable energy acceleration data from 2015-2016.

**Source Documentation:**
- GE assumed wrongly that "demand for natural gas and coal would continue to track global economic growth"
- This assumption was stated in strategic rationale for the 2014 Alstom acquisition ($13.5 billion deal announced April 30, 2014)
- The assumption persisted through Immelt's leadership until Flannery took over August 2017

**Report Title:** "General Electric Misread the Energy Transition: A Cautionary Tale"

**Status:** 403 access, but referenced in multiple news sources

**Key Documented Claims:**
- Immelt and GE Power CEO Steve Bolze failed to anticipate the speed of renewable energy adoption
- March 2017: GE forecast new plant orders of ~78 GW for 2017-2026 (later severely revised downward)

## Power Generation Market Data (Context)

### Gas Turbine Market Decline Timeline
- **2012-2013:** Combined cycle gas turbine capacity peaked, then declined
- **2012-2013:** Industry publications tracking decline; competitors (Siemens, Mitsubishi Hitachi Power Systems) undertaking major restructuring
- **2013:** GE's own competitors were restructuring while GE remained confident
- **2014:** GE Power & Water revenues still growing (+22% revenue, +13% earnings), masking market structural shift via backlog lag
- **2015:** Renewable energy scaling accelerated; GE completed Alstom acquisition betting on gas turbine future
- **March 2017:** GE forecast ~78 GW new gas plant orders 2017-2026
- **November 2017:** GE revised forecast DOWN 75% to ~40 units (from 160-unit forecast in March)

### Renewable Energy Data (Public & Available Pre-Decision)
- **2015:** Renewable electricity = 64% of U.S. capacity additions (vs. 52% in 2014)
- **2016:** Renewables = 61.5% of all new U.S. capacity
- **2015:** Solar generation UP 35.8%; wind UP 5.1%
- **2015:** Global renewable capacity additions = 152 GW (highest annual growth on record)
- **Source:** IRENA, EIA, NREL (all published 2015-2016, public data)

## H2-C Attack Sequence Evidence

### Recognition vs. Implication vs. Assumption Revision

**Pattern Analysis (Key for H2-C Test):**

1. **Recognition Level:** Immelt and team acknowledged renewable energy growth
   - Q1 2016: Renewable energy orders UP 86% (excl. Alstom)
   - 2014-2015: Renewable growth visible in multiple earnings calls
   
2. **Implication Level:** Did Immelt synthesize this into "renewable growth structurally reduces gas turbine demand"?
   - **Evidence of Gap:** December 2014 "The Pivot" presentation maintained "gas generation stable long-term"
   - **Evidence of Gap:** April 2014 Alstom acquisition rationale assumed gas/coal demand would track GDP
   - **Evidence of Gap:** March 2017 forecast for 78 GW new gas plants contradicted 2015-2016 renewable data
   
3. **Assumption Revision:** Did leadership update the operating model?
   - **No evidence found:** Through Immelt's leadership, no documented pivot from "gas stable in long-term" model
   - **Flannery took over August 2017:** First statement directly acknowledging "overcapacity, lower utilization... overall growth in renewables" (November 2017)
   - **180-degree shift:** Flannery's model explicitly contradicted Immelt's assumption

## Evidence Status for H2-C Hypothesis Attack

**Question:** Did Immelt already hold Flannery's model of power market transition before taking it over as CEO in August 2017?

**Current Evidence Summary:**

**Supporting H2-C (Information Failure Model):**
- Public renewable data (IRENA/EIA/NREL 2015-2016) was available before Alstom decision
- Immelt's model assumed gas/coal demand tracks GDP (contradicting public renewable acceleration)
- Q1 2017 assessment "fundamentals particularly strong" contradicted by Q2 results 90 days later
- No documented pivot from "gas stable long-term" to acknowledging renewable structural impact
- Flannery's November 2017 statements directly contradicted Immelt's assumptions

**Against H2-C (Immelt already held revised model):**
- Renewable orders growing substantially (Q1 2016: +86%)
- Immelt invested in renewable energy expansion (Haliade wind turbines)
- Still seeking evidence that Immelt internally held Flannery's model while publicly maintaining the old one

**Gap:** Direct quotes from Immelt's private statements, board presentations, or internal assessments needed to close the loop.
