## Key Evidence Summary

### Core Assumption (Contradicts Public Data)
**"Demand for natural gas and coal would track global economic growth"**
- Stated in: April 2014 Alstom acquisition rationale
- Contradicted by: IRENA/EIA/NREL data showing renewable capacity at 61-64% of new additions (2015-2016)
- Status: Persisted in Immelt model through Q2 2017
- Rejection: Flannery explicitly contradicted this in November 2017 statements

### Recognition vs. Implication Gap (H2-C Test)
**What Immelt Acknowledged:**
- ✓ Renewable energy growing (Q1 2016: +86% orders)
- ✓ Renewable cost declines 15-30% (IEEFA analysis)
- ✓ Renewable infrastructure investments (Haliade turbines)

**What Immelt Did NOT Integrate:**
- ✗ No statement connecting renewable growth to gas turbine demand destruction
- ✗ Maintained "gas stable long-term" positioning in 2014-2016
- ✗ Q1 2017 "fundamentals strong" contradicted by Q2 results 90 days later
- ✗ March 2017 forecast for 78 GW new gas plants contradicted by November revision to ~40 units

### Model Divergence Evidence (Flannery Comparison)
**Immelt Model (through August 2017):**
- Demand tracks GDP
- Gas generation stable long-term
- Power fundamentals particularly strong (Q1 2017)
- Alstom acquisition excellent value (15-20% ROI expected)

**Flannery Model (November 2017):**
- Traditional power markets have softened
- Volumes down significantly
- Overcapacity
- Growth driven by renewables (not gas)
- Market structural shift (not temporary cyclical weakness)

**Interpretation:** 3-month gap between Immelt departure (August) and Flannery assessment (November) suggests MODEL CHANGE, not new external data. Data available to Immelt was the same data available to Flannery.

## Notes for H2-C Investigation

**Core Finding:**
The evidence supports the interpretation that Immelt maintained the "demand tracks GDP" model despite publicly available renewable energy acceleration data contradicting it. The 90-day reversal between Q1 2017's "fundamentals strong" (+39% profit) and Q2 2017's collapse (-10% profit, -67% cash flow) suggests corporate leadership was not integrated with divisional reality.

**Key Diagnostic:**
- Recognition: Immelt acknowledged renewable growth ✓
- Implication: No evidence Immelt stated renewable growth would destroy gas turbine demand ?
- Assumption Revision: No evidence Immelt abandoned "demand tracks GDP" model before Flannery ✗

**Remaining Question:**
Was the gap between Q1 confidence and Q2 collapse a sign of:
1. **H2-C (Information Integration Failure):** Corporate missed signals that were available at divisional level
2. **External Shock (H4):** Market conditions changed rapidly between quarters (less likely — forecasts showed declining demand visibility since 2015)
3. **Deliberate Accounting (H3/C):** LTSA non-cash benefits masked underlying deterioration (partially confirmed by SEC settlement)

**Investigation Direction:**
Full Q2 2017 transcript and board presentations would clarify whether the gap was information asymmetry (H2-C) or accounting mechanism (H3). Current evidence suggests combination of both.
