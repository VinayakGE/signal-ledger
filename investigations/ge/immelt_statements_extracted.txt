Q1 2017 EARNINGS CALL - April 21, 2017
=========================================
"Power segment fundamentals particularly strong" (+39% profit YoY)

IMMELT STATEMENT:
- Power segment profit: UP 39% YoY
- Quality: High confidence expressed by management
- Industrial revenue: UP 4%

CASH FLOW ISSUES MASKED:
- Negative industrial cash flow from operations: -$1.6B
- Excuse: "Mainly timing related to collecting large accounts receivable balances"
- [Analysis: This was also related to LTSA factoring arrangements with GE Capital]

CRITICAL SIGNAL MISSED: 90 days later Q2 2017 showed massive reversal

STATUS: This represents the PEAK of Immelt's confidence in power fundamentals.
Only 90 days separated this +39% YoY statement from Q2 2017's -10% decline.

---

Q2 2017 EARNINGS CALL - July 21, 2017 [FINAL CALL UNDER IMMELT]
================================================

DRAMATIC REVERSAL IN 90 DAYS:
- Industrial segment operating profit: DOWN 4% YoY (vs. implied growth in April)
- Power segment profit: DOWN 10% YoY (vs. +39% just 90 days prior)
- Power segment revenue: UP 5% but profit DOWN 10% (margin compression)

OVERALL FINANCIAL DETERIORATION:
- Total net profit: DOWN 58% ($1.34B vs. $3.30B prior year)
- Total revenue: DOWN 12% YoY (fell to 19-month lows)
- Cash flow from operations: DOWN 67% ($3.6B vs. $10.9B prior year)
- Stock: Hit 19-month lows

IMMELT'S NARRATIVE:
- John Flannery (incoming CEO) was present on call
- This was Immelt's final earnings call as CEO
- Last opportunity to provide forward guidance before departure

CRITICAL ANALYSIS POINT:
The Q1-Q2 2017 reversal is EVIDENCE FOR H2-C hypothesis:
- Q1 assessment: "fundamentals particularly strong"
- Q2 reality: Dramatic profit decline, margin compression, cash flow collapse
- Interpretation: Corporate leadership's assessment (Q1) did not match divisional reality (Q2)
- This supports claim that information integration failed (H2-C) rather than signals were absent

Immelt: "Alstom not only advances our strategic priorities and industrial growth, but is also expected to provide an excellent return on capital"

CAPITAL RETURNS PROJECTION:
- Expected ROI: 15-20%

- Assumption: This mix would remain stable and predictable

COMPETITIVE POSITIONING:
- "Complementary technology, global capability, installed base, and talent"

FLANNERY REPLACEMENT MODEL - November 2017 (Post-Immelt)
=========================================================

Direct contradiction of Immelt's model:

Flannery Statement (November 2017):
"Traditional power markets including gas and coal have softened. Volumes are down significantly... overcapacity, lower utilization... overall growth in renewables"

Key Differences from Immelt Model:
1. IMMELT: Demand tracks global economic growth
   FLANNERY: Renewables structurally reducing gas/coal demand

2. IMMELT: Gas generation "stable in long-term"
   FLANNERY: Overcapacity, utilization declining

3. IMMELT: Power fundamentals "particularly strong" (Q1 2017)
   FLANNERY: Market "softened significantly" (Q3 2017)

CRITICAL FINDING: This 180-degree model shift occurred BETWEEN Immelt's August 2017 departure and Flannery's first assessment in November 2017 (3 months). No new market data occurred in those 3 months. The data available to Immelt was the same data available to Flannery—it was the MODEL that changed, not the external reality.

==========================================

WHAT WE HAVE (Recognition):
✓ Renewable energy acceleration visible in public data (IRENA/EIA/NREL 2015-2016)
✓ Q1 2016: Renewable orders UP 86%
✓ Multiple earnings calls acknowledging renewable growth
✓ Immelt team investing in renewable energy (Haliade turbines)

WHAT WE NEED (Implication):
? Evidence that Immelt explicitly stated: "Renewable acceleration structurally reduces gas turbine demand"
? Evidence that leadership understood renewable growth as PRIMARY driver of market structure change

WHAT WE LACK (Assumption Revision):
✗ No documented revision of "gas demand tracks GDP" assumption
✗ Q1 2017 "fundamentals strong" contradicted only by Q2 results, not by prior public revision of model
✗ Flannery's November 2017 model divergence suggests model CHANGE, not prior knowledge

CONCLUSION TO TEST:
If Immelt held Flannery's model internally but maintained "gas stable" position publicly → H2-C weakens
If Immelt genuinely held "gas demand tracks GDP" model until August 2017 → H2-C strengthens

Current evidence points toward GENUINE model difference, not disclosure lag.
But full Q2 2017 and board presentation transcripts needed to close the test.
