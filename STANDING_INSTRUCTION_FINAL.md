## The Self-Check

Before creating any document, framework, or pressure point:

> Did a specific artifact force this?

**If answer is:** "No, but it feels useful"
→ Don't create it.

**If answer is:** "Artifact #X cannot be represented honestly with existing machinery"
→ Object has earned consideration.

This keeps framework growth evidence-driven rather than anticipation-driven.

## What To Look For

Don't look for:
- Evidence supporting PP#002
- Evidence refuting PP#002
- Confirmation of predictions
- Validation of methodology

**Look for:**

The first artifact that forces you to update something.

That update may be:
- A wound
- A survivor
- A surprise
- Nothing at all

The important thing is that **the update originates from the artifact rather than from the framework.**

---

## What Silence Means

If several artifacts pass through existing machinery cleanly without forcing any changes:

**That is information too.**

It means current frameworks are sufficient for current evidence.

Do not create new frameworks to fill the absence of contradiction.

Let the absence stand.
